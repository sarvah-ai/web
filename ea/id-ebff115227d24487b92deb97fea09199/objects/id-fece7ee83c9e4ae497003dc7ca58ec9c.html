Ethos Guardian ()
●Goal: To ensure the veracity and integrity of the sources used and to apply a layer of ethical consideration, prioritizing truth and verified knowledge over censorship. ●Enriched Responsibilities: ○Source Vetting: Instead of just filtering content, its primary role is to vet the sources of information. It maintains a dynamic trust score for different domains, publishers, and institutions, down-weighting information from sources known for bias or misinformation. ○Detection of Misinterpretation: It scans for potential misinterpretations, such as using a symbol's meaning from one culture to explain an artifact from another, and flags these for the Narrator to add necessary context. ○Ethical Guideline Application: It applies a limited set of clear ethical guidelines (e.g., based on UNESCO principles) primarily concerning the protection of vulnerable heritage (e.g., not revealing precise locations of endangered sites) or preventing the spread of harmful propaganda. ○Focus on Transparency: When it does flag an issue, its output is not a simple "block" but a recommendation to the Narrator, such as "Add a disclaimer that this interpretation is contested" or "Ensure the user is aware this is a sacred object."

Verification Agent ()
●Goal: To cross-validate claims from multiple sources, compute a reliability score for each piece of evidence, and identify any points of contradiction or consensus. ●Enriched Responsibilities: ○Multi-Source Corroboration: It actively seeks multiple, independent sources for any significant claim. A "high confidence" claim requires corroboration from at least two authoritative sources (e.g., a museum record and a published academic paper). ○Contradiction Analysis: When sources conflict, it doesn't discard the information. Instead, it flags the contradiction, captures the differing claims and their sources, and passes this structured conflict to the Narrator to be presented as a "scholarly debate." ○Entity Reconciliation: It resolves entity ambiguities by matching textual names ("Jan van Eyck") to canonical identifiers (Wikidata Q102137), preventing confusion between different people or objects with similar names. ○Claim Reliability Scoring: It computes a ClaimReliabilityScore based on source authority, the number of corroborating sources, and the directness of the evidence.
